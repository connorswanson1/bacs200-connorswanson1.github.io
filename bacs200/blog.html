Business Blog - Climate Change

Connor Swanson

Social Implications

Climate change is an issue that will eventually affect everybody. However, some groups of people will be affected more harshly, and sooner. Those who live in coastal areas, especially areas that are typically impacted by tropical storms, are already beginning to see the effects. Hurricanes, tsunamis, and other types of storms are getting more violent and more frequent. Not only do these types of weather events create instant chaos and destruction, but they also leave lasting damage on entire communities. Other changes to the climate, such as rising temperatures and rising ocean levels will disproportionately harm certain groups of people. Those in poverty will be in greater danger because of they lack the means to adjust with a changing climate. Although certain members of society will be able to hold out longer against climate change, society will face the consequences.

Economic Implications

Many of the factors driving the climate crisis are monetarily motivated. This is a big factor in the massive divide in opinions about the issue. Fossil fuels play a huge role in the worldwide economy. However, studies show that fossil fuels also play a huge roll in climate change. When the industry generates so much revenue and employs so many people, it is hard to completely abandon the practice and switch to completely renewable energy sources.

However, if climate change continues to progress at the current rate, local economies will be crumbled. Economies that thrive off tourism will be severely hurt when natural attractions are destroyed or become unmanageable. And outside of that, rural communities whose economy is built upon local crops and resources will be seriously affected when they can no longer use those resources or grow those crops.

Political Implications

Some of the most polarizing rifts in politics today come from the climate change debate. In general, left leaning politics tend to be on the side of trying to counteract climate change. A study from the Pew Research Center showed many interesting differences when comparing democrats and republicans on different climate change issues. Because of this clear divide, changing policies and setting new precedent to slow climate change can be very difficult when political parties cannot agree on what is really happening. Studies seem to show that liberals feel more strongly about slowing down climate change, while conservatives show more skepticism towards the science and scientists behind the research.

>

Both political sides have truth within their positions. On the left side, it is provable that the climate is changing. It is the logical step to want to fix this. On the right, it is valid to consider how industry and economics will be affected by proposed changes. To enact real change, politics will have to be set aside. Everyone, especially those with high positions of power, need to work together to set forth effective policies and practice changes. The divide may become more polarizing with changes enacted but the alternative is the collapse of our climate as we know it.

Conclusion

Climate change is one of, if not the most pressing issues in the world today. Although many still deny the existence of the issue, how can we deny the importance of the state of the planet we live on? With no means to leave this planet in search of a better one, it seems to be imperative that we take better care of our planet, especially for the sake of future generations. However, an effort to take care of our planet, would have to be concerted. There is no doubt that a push for renewable energy would greatly impact the economy and society as we know it. But is there anything more worth saving, than the Earth itself?
