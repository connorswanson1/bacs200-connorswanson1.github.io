Business Blog - Climate Change

Connor Swanson

Social Implications

Climate change is an issue that will eventually affect everybody. However, some groups of people will be affected more harshly, and sooner. Those who live in coastal areas, especially areas that are typically impacted by tropical storms, are already begining to see the effects. Hurricanes, tsunamis, and other types of storms are getting more violent and more frequent. Not only do these types of weather events create instant chaos and destruction, they leave lasting damage on entire communities. Other changes to the climate, such as rising temperatures and rising ocean levels will disproportionately harm certain groups of people. Those in poverty will be in greater danger because of they lack the means to adjust with a changing climate. Although certain members of society will be able to hold out longer against climte change, society as a whole will face the consequences.

Economic Implications

Many of the factors driving the climate crisis are monetarily motivated. This is a big factor in the massive divide in opions about the issue. Fossil fuels play a huge role in the worldwide economy. However, studies show that fossil fuels also play a huge roll in climate change. When the industry generates so much revenue and employs so many people, it is pretty hard to completley abandon the practice and switch to completely renewable energy sources.

However, if climate change continues to progress at the current rate, local economies will be crumbled. Economies that thrive off of tourism will be severly hurt when natural attractions are destroyed or become unmanagable. And outside of that, rural communities whose economy is built upon local crops and resources will be seriously affected when they can no longer use those resources or grow those crops.

Political Implications

Some of the most polarizing rifts in politics today come from the climate change debate.

Conclusion

Climate change is one of, if not the most pressing issues in the world today. Although many still deny the existence of the issue, how can we deny the importance of the state of the planet we live on? With no means to leave this planet in search of a better one, it seems to be imperative that we take better care of our planet, especially for the sake of future generations. However, an effort to take care of our planet, would have to be concerted. There is no doubt that a push for renewable energy would greatly impact the economy and society as we know it. But is there anything more worth saving, than the Earth itself?
